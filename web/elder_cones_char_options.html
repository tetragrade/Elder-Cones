Character Creation Steps

read the full rules here

  1. Stats & Facets

    Assign stats and Facets, and decide your character's name, appearance, etc.
    Your stats are Light & Flesh. You start with 4 Light, 4 Flesh, and 4 points to assign freely.

    Facets are descriptors of character's drives and talents. To compare them to something else: they're sort of like skills, or Fate's aspects.
    You start with two Facets, both of die size d6.

    d6Example Facets
    Personality Ideology Profession Hobby
    1 studious god-fearing alchemist falconer
    2 short fuse imperial loyalist priest duellist
    3 glutton alien worshipper acrobat pan flautist
    4 brave hermeticist thief drummer
    5 humble leveller astrologer card shark
    6 calm elven supremacist hunter poet

  2. Choose One Boon

    Wizarding Degree

    Wizards cast free-form spells, assembling them on the fly.

    • You are fully literate in the language of magic.
    • You start with a number of Grimoires equal to your Light, you decide which words they contain.
      Your character has a chance to read their Grimoires before the start of the session, memorize any number of words from them.
      They are stored in your room at the local inn, or on your person: you decide.
    Item NameDescription
    Grimoire Read to memorize the magic word written inside. Reading a grimoire takes one hour.

    Psionic Power

    Psions replicate preset effects with the power of their minds.

    • You start with 1 PK Power, it fills a Light Slot.
    Item NameDescription
    PK ??? Choose any first-level or level-less spell (B/X or general OSR), and add PK in front of it.
    The power replicates the spell. Once used, it becomes unusable until the next scene.

    Experienced Warrior

    You know how it is.

    • Enemies save vs your attacks at +4 Dif (+20% hit chance).

    Trained Thief

    From each according to my ability, to me.

    • You speak a special rhyming language understood only by the precariat.
    • You are usually carrying a set of odd items, i.e. tubing and hairpins, which you can turn into thieving tools on the spot. They cost no inventory space, and only trained coppers know their true purpose.
    • So long as you follow the Thieves Guild code, you ignore the stress of Conventions against crime.

  3. Pick Equipment

    You can take as many items as you can carry, each item fills a Flesh Slot.
    These are suggested categories. If you have an idea that isn't listed, ask me about it. We'll make it work.

    Item NameDescription
    Mundane Melee Weapon i.e. sword, mace, spear. d6 damage.
    Mundane Ranged Weapon i.e. bow, crossbow. d6 damage.
    Range 500-ft, defenders Save at -10 Dif if you are engaged in melee, or in a tight space.
    Mundane Armor Piece i.e. helmet, pauldron, breastplate, shield. Grants +4 HP
    Torch 40-ft radius. Once lit: 1-in-10 each turn to extinguish.
    Tool Kit Contains any small item that you need to perform the named task.
    i.e. Lock-Picking Kit, Blacksmith's Kit, Climbing Kit.
    Using an appropriate tool grants a -3 modifier to Saves.
    Large tool A large tool. i.e. Pickaxe, Sledgehammer.
    Using an appropriate tool grants a -3 modifier to Saves.
    Bag of Money Worth 1 day's Room & Board. Big $ Sign on front.
